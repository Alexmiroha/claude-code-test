# How to check if a discount is real

Not every promotion is a good deal. Sometimes a store raises the price first and then shows a large discount later. Before buying, it is worth checking whether the price cut is actually real.

## 1. Check the price history

The most important question is: **how much did this product cost before?**

Check:

- the lowest price from the last 30 days,
- the price from a few weeks or months ago,
- the typical market price,
- the price at other sellers.

If a product used to cost 999 PLN, then suddenly went up to 1299 PLN, and now is “on sale” for 999 PLN, that is not a real deal.

## 2. Compare the price across several stores

Do not buy only because you see **-30%**. It is better to check how much the same product costs elsewhere.

Compare:

- Allegro,
- Amazon,
- x-kom,
- Morele,
- Media Expert,
- RTV Euro AGD,
- Komputronik,
- the manufacturer’s official store.

Sometimes a product with a “huge discount” in one store is simply cheaper as a standard price somewhere else.

## 3. Check the delivery cost

The product price is not everything. What matters is the **final price**.

Before buying, check:

- delivery cost,
- return cost,
- delivery time,
- warranty terms,
- whether the seller is local or foreign.

A product for 499 PLN with expensive delivery may be worse value than a product for 519 PLN with free delivery and easy returns.

## 4. Watch out for fake discounts

The most common warning signs:

- a very high crossed-out price,
- no price history,
- an unknown seller,
- a suspiciously large discount,
- no proper warranty,
- a description full of generic marketing phrases,
- a product listing without clear specifications.

A big discount does not automatically mean a good deal.

## 5. Check reviews, but carefully

Reviews can help, but they are not always fully reliable.

Pay attention to:

- the number of reviews,
- review dates,
- repeated problems,
- photos from buyers,
- seller ratings,
- negative comments.

If a product has only perfect reviews and they all sound similar, be careful.

## Quick checklist before buying

Before clicking “buy now”, check:

- Is the price lower than usual?
- Do other stores have a similar or higher price?
- Does delivery ruin the deal?
- Is the seller reliable?
- Does the product have a normal warranty?
- Do you really need this product now?

## Summary

A real discount is not the biggest percentage off. A real discount is **a good price compared to price history, the market and the product’s real value**.

# Buy now or wait?

This is one of the most common questions in online shopping. The price looks good, the promotion ends today, and the store shows a message like **“last units available”**. But is it really worth buying right now?

## When it makes sense to buy now

Buying now makes sense if at least two or three of these conditions are true:

- the price is the lowest it has been in a long time,
- the product has limited availability,
- you need it now,
- the product rarely goes on sale,
- the difference versus the usual price is really meaningful,
- the seller is reliable,
- returns and warranty are straightforward.

If the price is good, the product is needed and the seller is trustworthy, buying now can be a reasonable decision.

## When it is better to wait

It is better to wait if:

- the product is not urgent,
- the promotion looks average,
- the price often drops,
- Black Friday, Cyber Monday or a seasonal sale is coming soon,
- a new model is expected soon,
- the product has mixed reviews,
- you are not sure whether you really need it.

The worst purchases are the ones we make only because a promotion is “ending soon”.

## Products that often get cheaper

Some categories regularly drop in price.

This often applies to:

- electronics,
- smartphones,
- laptops,
- TVs,
- headphones,
- computer accessories,
- small home appliances,
- seasonal clothing,
- gaming products.

With these products, patience often pays off.

## Products worth buying when the price is good

Some products do not always get large discounts. If you find a genuinely good price, it can make sense to buy faster.

Examples:

- popular models with strong reviews,
- products with limited availability,
- equipment that rarely gets discounted,
- everyday essentials,
- products you already planned to buy.

In these cases, waiting for the perfect deal may not make sense.

## The simple 24-hour rule

If you are not sure, use the 24-hour rule.

Do not buy immediately. Add the product to your cart or wishlist and come back the next day.

After 24 hours, ask yourself:

- Do I still want this product?
- Do I really need it?
- Does the price still look good?
- Have I found a better offer?
- Am I buying the product, or just the emotion of the promotion?

This is a simple way to avoid impulse purchases.

## Quick checklist

Buy now if:

- the price is genuinely good,
- you need the product,
- the seller is reliable,
- return terms are good,
- you checked other stores.

Wait if:

- the product is not urgent,
- the price is not exceptional,
- the promotion looks artificial,
- bigger sales may be coming soon,
- you are not sure about the purchase.

## Summary

The best shopping decision is not always “buy now”. Sometimes the best deal is **not buying anything under pressure**.

# Outlet, open-box and refurbished — when are they worth it?

Outlet, open-box and refurbished products can be much cheaper than new ones. Sometimes they are a great deal, and sometimes they are only an apparent saving. The key is to understand exactly what you are buying.

## What is outlet?

Outlet usually means a product that is:

- returned by a customer,
- used as a display unit,
- sold with damaged packaging,
- sold with minor signs of use,
- from an end-of-line batch,
- briefly used by a previous buyer.

Outlet does not always mean damaged. But you should always read the condition description carefully.

## What is open-box?

Open-box usually means that the packaging has been opened.

It may be:

- a customer return,
- a product checked by the store,
- a device without original foil,
- a complete product that has already been unpacked.

Open-box can be a very good option if the product has a full warranty and a clear condition description.

## What is refurbished?

Refurbished means a product that has been renewed or restored for resale.

It may be:

- technically checked,
- repaired,
- cleaned,
- tested,
- sold with a limited or full warranty.

With refurbished products, it is very important to know who renewed the product: the manufacturer, an authorized service center or an external seller.

## When is outlet or open-box worth buying?

It makes sense when:

- the price difference is large,
- the seller describes the condition clearly,
- the product has warranty,
- returns are easy,
- minor signs of use do not bother you,
- the product is not extremely delicate or risky.

Best outlet categories:

- monitors,
- keyboards,
- mice,
- headphones,
- business laptops,
- computer accessories,
- small home appliances,
- office equipment.

## When is it better to avoid outlet?

Be more careful with products where technical condition matters a lot.

Be cautious with:

- batteries and power banks,
- smartphones with unknown history,
- SSD and HDD drives,
- heavily worn mechanical equipment,
- products without warranty,
- products without return options,
- equipment described in very vague terms.

If the description says “the product may show signs of use” but there are no photos or details, be careful.

## What to check before buying

Before buying, check:

- the exact product condition,
- photos of the actual item,
- included accessories,
- warranty length,
- return option,
- seller reviews,
- whether the price is much lower than a new product.

If the saving is only 5–10%, it is often better to buy new.

## Good deal or risk?

Outlet is a good deal if:

- you save a meaningful amount,
- the product has a clear condition description,
- you have warranty and returns,
- the seller is reliable.

Outlet is risky if:

- the description is unclear,
- there are no photos,
- there is no warranty,
- the price is not much lower,
- the product has parts strongly affected by wear.

## Summary

Outlet, open-box and refurbished products can be a great way to save money. But only when the price is genuinely good and the purchase conditions are safe.

The simplest rule: **the higher the product risk, the bigger the saving should be**.
